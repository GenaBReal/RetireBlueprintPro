📚 Key Terms Glossary
4% Rule — Withdraw 4% of portfolio per year for a sustainable 30-year retirement
SWR — Safe Withdrawal Rate: annual withdrawal ÷ portfolio size
FIRE — Financial Independence, Retire Early: retire when portfolio sustains expenses
Coast FIRE — Portfolio large enough to reach FI without more contributions
RMD — Required Minimum Distribution: mandatory IRA/401k withdrawals starting at age 73
IRMAA — Medicare income surcharge triggered when income exceeds $212K (2025, MFJ)
Roth Conversion — Moving pre-tax money to Roth; pay tax now, grow tax-free forever
COLA — Cost of Living Adjustment: annual SS benefit increase (historically 2-3%/yr)
Pre-Tax Account — Traditional 401k/IRA: contributions reduce taxes now, taxed at withdrawal
Roth Account — Contributions are after-tax; all growth and withdrawals are tax-free
HSA — Health Savings Account: triple tax advantage (pre-tax in, grows tax-free, tax-free medical out)
Sequence Risk — Danger of a market crash early in retirement while withdrawing; permanently reduces portfolio
Smile Curve — Spending pattern: high early (active), lower mid (settled), higher late (healthcare)
FRA — Full Retirement Age for Social Security: typically age 66-67 depending on birth year
Survivor Benefit — When spouse dies, survivor keeps the higher of the two SS benefits
Legacy Goal — Target ending portfolio balance to leave as inheritance or estate
