📖 Sequence of Returns Risk: A $925K portfolio dropping 20% in Year 1 = $740K before any withdrawal. Then $87K withdrawn = $653K. That $272K gap compounds against you for 50 years. The same crash in Year 20 barely affects you — your portfolio had 20 years to grow first. Your three defenses: (1) Guaranteed income floor (SS + pension), (2) Roth allocation, (3) Spending flexibility.
🎲 Monte Carlo Simulation
1,000 randomized market scenarios · Runs entirely in your browser · No re-query needed
What Monte Carlo simulation means: Your Dashboard shows ONE projected future based on your expected return. But the real market is unpredictable — some years it returns 30%, others it drops 40%. Monte Carlo runs 1,000 completely different versions of your retirement, each with random annual returns drawn from realistic market distributions, then counts how many scenarios end with your portfolio still above your legacy goal.

Why this matters: Two people can have identical plans and identical average returns over 30 years — but if one retires into a bear market and the other into a bull market, their outcomes are completely different. This is called sequence of returns risk and it's the #1 reason "average returns" don't tell the whole story.

✅ 90%+ = Very strong · ⚠️ 75–89% = Acceptable, can strengthen · ❌ Below 75% = Needs adjustment
The simulation uses your actual portfolio balance, spending, SS income, pension, and life expectancy — not generic numbers.
