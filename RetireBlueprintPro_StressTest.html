📖 What is Sequence of Returns Risk? The biggest retirement threat isn't a bad market — it's a bad market at the WRONG TIME. A $925K portfolio dropping 20% in Year 1 becomes $740K before you withdraw anything. Then you withdraw $87K for living expenses = $653K. That $272K gap compounds against you for 50 years. The same crash in Year 20 barely affects you — your portfolio had 20 years to grow first.

Your three defenses: (1) Guaranteed income floor — SS + pension reduces how much you need from the portfolio. (2) Roth allocation — Roth grows tax-free and has no RMDs; more Roth = more flexibility. (3) Spending flexibility — cutting 10-15% for 2-3 years after a crash is the single most powerful move.
