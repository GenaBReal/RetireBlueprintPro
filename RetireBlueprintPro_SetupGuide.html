Entering Your Information
Inputs tab field reference · all sections explained
All your personal information goes in the Inputs tab. This is the only tab you ever need to edit. The Master, Tax Engine, and Annual Check-In tabs update automatically from formulas.
Golden Rule
Only edit cells that are WHITE. Gray or colored cells contain formulas. Editing them will break your calculations. If you see a #REF! error: go to File → Settings → Calculation, ensure Iterative Calculation is ON, Max iterations = 50, Threshold = 0.05.
How to Unhide Tabs
Your Google Sheet shows only the Master tab by default — all other tabs are hidden to keep things clean. To unhide any tab: click the ≡ (three lines) icon at the very bottom left of your Google Sheet → a list of hidden tabs appears → click the tab name you want → click Show. The Inputs tab is the most useful one to unhide if you want to verify your data or enter numbers directly into the sheet instead of using the web form.

How the Model Works
The engine behind every number
RetireBlueprint Pro is built on layered, real-world financial logic — not broad annual estimates. Understanding how it works helps you trust every number.
Master Engine
The Master tab contains a single 15,000+ character ARRAYFORMULA in cell A8. This formula uses Google Sheets' LET(), SEQUENCE(), VSTACK(), HSTACK(), and MAKEARRAY() functions to generate the entire 50-year projection table in one pass. Each row represents one year. All 76 columns — income, expenses, taxes, withdrawals, account balances, RMDs, Roth conversions, and ending net worth — are calculated simultaneously with no duplicate logic.
Pro-Rated Timing
This model does not assume life happens on January 1st. Income starts and stops at exact dates. Retirement is applied mid-year when appropriate. Social Security begins in the exact month selected. This creates precise, real-world cash flow accuracy that most retirement planners cannot match.
Tax-Aware Withdrawals — The Gross-Up Principle
Most plans underestimate withdrawals by ignoring taxes. This model works backward from your actual spending need. If you need $1,000 to pay bills and owe 12% in taxes, the model calculates that you must withdraw $1,136.36 — so that after paying $136.36 in tax, you have exactly $1,000. Your lifestyle is never underfunded due to tax drag.
Smile Curve Spending
Spending naturally changes over retirement. Go-Go Years (higher activity, travel), Slow-Go Years (moderated lifestyle), No-Go Years (lower activity, higher care costs). The model applies phase weights automatically across your projection. This feature can be turned on or off, and each phase's year range and multiplier is fully editable.
Two-Life & Survivor Logic
The model tracks both partners independently and together. Income stops exactly at each projected death year. Household expenses drop by your chosen survivor reduction percentage after the first partner passes. All calculations stop at the second partner's projected death year. No overestimation. No phantom growth beyond real life.
Clean Ending Logic
The model ends when the plan ends. No artificial extensions. No inflated projections beyond your plan horizon. Just a clear, accurate financial endpoint at your legacy target — with a variance of ±$1 accuracy from the solver.
Key Variables to Monitor
  • Inflation Rate — drives long-term expenses more than almost any other variable. 1% difference over 30 years is enormous.
  • Social Security COLA — adjusts guaranteed income over time. The 2.5% default is the historical average.
  • Retirement Timing — one of the most powerful levers in your plan. One extra year of work can add years of runway.
  • Healthcare Inflation — defaults to 5.3% which is historically accurate but worth monitoring.
  • Expected Returns — the model uses straight-line growth. Real markets are volatile. Use the Bear Market stress test to see downside scenarios.

Understanding the Formulas
Plain English explanations of every key metric
Plan Confidence Score
Combines three factors: (1) whether your legacy goal is met, (2) whether your withdrawal rate is below the safe threshold of 4.25%, and (3) how many years of runway your plan produces. Above 90% is Very Strong. 75–90% is Strong. Below 75% needs attention and a planning review.
Safe Spending Capacity — The Solver
Calculated by an iterative formula that finds the maximum annual base extra spend that still allows your portfolio to meet the legacy goal. It runs thousands of calculations to find this number to within $1 of accuracy. This is the single most important number in your plan — it tells you exactly how much you can safely spend beyond your base lifestyle every year.
Withdrawal Rate
Your first-year withdrawal divided by your starting portfolio value. Research known as the Trinity Study suggests 4% is sustainable over 30 years. Your plan targets a rate below 4.25% as a conservative threshold. Your dashboard shows your actual rate and whether it falls in the safe zone.
Roth Conversion Savings
Shows how much you save in lifetime taxes by converting pre-tax money to Roth now versus waiting until Required Minimum Distributions begin at age 73. The calculation compares your current marginal tax rate to your assumed future rate when RMDs force withdrawals. If this number is positive and significant — act now while your rate is low.
Gross-Up Withdrawal
The amount you must actually withdraw from accounts to net the spending amount you need after taxes. If you need $65,000 net and your effective rate is 12%, you must withdraw $73,864 gross. The difference goes to taxes. Your plan always calculates the gross amount — your lifestyle is never underfunded.
Survivor Income Score
After the first partner passes, income drops (one Social Security check instead of two) and expenses drop by your survivor reduction percentage. This score shows how well the remaining income covers the reduced expenses. Above 90% means the surviving partner is financially well protected for the full remainder of the plan.
Withdrawal Sequencing
The model draws accounts in tax-optimal order: Taxable brokerage first (captures preferential LTCG rates while income is still low), then Pre-Tax accounts (manages RMD exposure), Roth last (tax-free, grows longest). This sequencing is calculated automatically by the Master engine every year.

Frequently Asked Questions
Common questions answered
Is this financial advice?
No. RetireBlueprint Pro is a planning and visualization tool. The numbers are projections based on your inputs and assumptions — they are not guarantees. Always review your plan with a licensed financial advisor before making major decisions about retirement timing, Social Security claiming, or large Roth conversions.
How accurate are the projections?
As accurate as your inputs and assumptions. The model uses straight-line growth rates and does not account for year-to-year market volatility. Use the Bear Market stress test in the Inputs tab to see how your plan holds up in adverse market conditions.
What if I see a #REF! or circular error?
This model uses iterative calculations to solve taxes and withdrawals simultaneously. Go to File → Settings → Calculation, ensure Iterative Calculation is ON, Max iterations = 50, Threshold = 0.05. This resolves the issue in almost all cases.
Can I add more accounts?
Yes. The Inputs tab supports up to 12 named accounts. Fill in additional rows in Section VI with the account name, owner, type, balance, expected return, and withdrawal settings.
What does Protected mean for an account?
A Protected account is excluded from the withdrawal calculation. The solver will never draw it down to fund living expenses. Useful for emergency funds, 529 college plans, or accounts earmarked for a specific purpose.
How do I run a scenario — like retiring 2 years earlier?
Change the Salary End Date for one or both partners in Section II/III of Inputs. The entire projection recalculates. You can also toggle the Early Bear Market or Late Bear Market stress tests in Section I to see downside scenarios. Always save a copy of your base case before running scenarios.
How do I share this with my financial advisor?
The dashboard HTML opens in any browser. Email it, share your screen in a meeting, or save as PDF via File → Print → Save as PDF in Chrome. It contains no passwords or sensitive credentials.
How do I update my dashboard after changing numbers?
Update your Inputs tab — the Google Sheet recalculates automatically. Then press Cmd+R (Mac) or Ctrl+R (Windows) in Chrome to refresh any of the HTML pages. All charts and numbers update instantly from your live sheet.
